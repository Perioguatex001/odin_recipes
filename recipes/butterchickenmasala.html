ButterChickenMasala


Home Page

Butter chicken, also known as murgh makhani, is a popular Indian dish featuring tender, grilled chicken in a rich, creamy tomato-based sauce. The sauce is typically flavored with butter, spices, and sometimes cream, giving it a smooth, velvety texture. It's a globally recognized dish, often found on Indian restaurant menus worldwide

Ingredients

  • Yogurt
  • Ginger Garlic Paste
  • Lemon Juice
  • Chilli Powder
  • Garam masala
  • salt
  • Tomato paste/sauce
  • Butter & Cream
  • Cubed chicken breast/ Thigh pieces
  • Coriander
  • Turmeric
  • Kasuri methi

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken pieces (500 gram) using 1 tbsp lemon juice, 1/4 teaspoon salt, 1/2 garam masala, 1 tbsp oil, 3/4 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 1/3 cup yogurt
  2. Make sure there is no excess moisture on your chicken. add red chilli powder, salt and lemon juice. mix well, cover and rest for 20 minutes.
  3. Later add ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, kasuri methi, turmeric and hung curd.
  4. Heat 1 to 2 tbsp oil in a pan and add 1.5 cups (130 gram) sliced onions and saute on a medium heat until slightly golden, for 7 to 8 minutes.
  5. Add 500 grams of sliced tomatoes, 28 whole cashews. Pour half cup water to the blender and grind to a smooth puree.
  6. Heat 2 tablespoons butter in a pan. On sizzling, add ginger garlic paste, green chilies and fry on a medium heat for 1 to 2 minutes till the raw smell goes away.
  7. Now pure the puree in it. If your puree is not smooth then strain it to the pan.
  8. Mix well and bring this to a boil on a medium high heat. Be careful as this can easily splatter.
  9. Cover partially and let boil on a medium heat, stirring often, until thick. This takes about 12 to 15 minutes, depending on the kind of coookware and stove.
  10. Pour 1.5 cups hot water and mix well.
  11. Simmer this, partially covered for 10 minutes, until the sauce thickens & traces of fats/butter are visible on top of the sauce.
  12. On a fry pan, add one tablespoon oil/butter or ghee and place the chicken pieces spacing them apart. Fry on a medium heat for 2 minutes. Ignore the moisture oozing out of the chicken. Don't burn and don't overcook.
  13. Cook till the chicken is just done and all the moisture evaporates away.
  14. Finally, add chicken to the butter chicken gravy.
  15. Pour 1/2 cup more hot water if the sauce is too thick and simmer for about 5 to 7 minutes till it becomes tender. This is the time the sauce actually develops the flavours.
  16. Add 1/2 to 3/4 teaspoon salt, 1 teaspoon sugar and 1/2 tablespoon kasuri methi. Stir well and taste test. If required add more garam masala & another tablespoon butter for additional flavour.
