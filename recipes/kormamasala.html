KormaMasala


Home Page

Korma Masala is a traditional Indian curry made with tender meat or vegetables cooked in a rich, spiced yogurt and nut-based sauce. It's known for its mildly spicy and creamy flavor profile, often enhanced with fragrant spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves. This royal dish is best served with naan or basmati rice.

Ingredients

  • Chicken or lamb pieces (500g)
  • Yogurt
  • Ginger garlic paste
  • Onions
  • Cashew nuts or almonds
  • Fresh cream
  • Green chilies
  • Cloves, cinnamon, cardamom
  • Coriander powder
  • Turmeric
  • Red chili powder
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves

Steps

  1. Marinate 500g chicken or lamb with 1 cup yogurt, 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp red chili powder, and salt. Let it rest for at least 1 hour.
  2. Heat 2 tbsp oil or ghee in a pan. Add whole spices – 2 cloves, 1-inch cinnamon stick, and 2 green cardamoms. Sauté for a few seconds.
  3. Add 2 finely sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  4. Grind 10-12 soaked cashews or almonds into a smooth paste and add to the pan. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add marinated meat and cook on medium heat until the meat is sealed and starts to brown.
  6. Add 1 tsp coriander powder and 1/2 tsp chili powder. Mix well.
  7. Cover and cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes or until the meat is tender. Add water if needed to avoid burning.
  8. Add 1/4 cup fresh cream and mix well. Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  9. Adjust salt and spice levels to taste. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with naan, paratha, or steamed rice.
